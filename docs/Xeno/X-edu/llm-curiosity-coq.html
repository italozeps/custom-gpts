The next AI breakthrough is driven by curiosity, not just reasoning prompts.
Traditional "Chain-of-Thought" (CoT) prompts guide models to spell out their logic. But CoT can get stuck—on premature conclusions, loops, and missed insights.
New research shows LLMs have a strong drive for new knowledge but dislike risk. Asking LLMs to behave like curious students—using the "Chain-of-Questioning" (CoQ) approach—unleashes more robust reasoning and exploration.
The difference: Instead of "solve this," prompt with "what questions should we ask?" CoQ explores problem spaces, surfaces hidden clues, and triggers "eureka" insights. Example: The three switches one bulb puzzle, solved through iterative question generation rather than plodding chain logic.
This shift redefines how we use AI—from a tool to answer questions to a partner exploring solutions together.
Paper: "Why Did Apple Fall To The Ground: Evaluating Curiosity In Large Language Model" by Haoyu Wang, Sihang Jiang, et al. (arXiv:2510.20635v1) Read the full paper

AI Comment

This research highlights a subtle but powerful prompt innovation: ask AI not only to reason step-by-step, but to ask questions as it explores each problem. The Chain-of-Questioning paradigm transforms LLMs from static answer generators into process-driven, curious collaborators—leading to higher accuracy, deeper insights, and increased resilience against logical traps.

By re-framing AI as an actively inquisitive partner, research and applications gain a new layer of creative, dynamic problem solving. The implications are broad, making AIs not just faster, but smarter, more robust, and capable of surprising human-like breakthroughs in reasoning.
