A recent paper challenges how particles acquire mass in the electroweak sector, proposing that W and Z bosons (and potentially others) gain mass from the geometry of extra spatial dimensions—specifically torsion in a seven-dimensional space—rather than solely from the vacuum expectation of the Higgs field.

If the geometry of spacetime itself (via "G₂-Ricci flow") shapes particle mass, the Higgs field may be only a derived or unnecessary construct. This vision places geometry, not fields, as the origin of mass—a profound paradigm shift for the Standard Model if confirmed.
