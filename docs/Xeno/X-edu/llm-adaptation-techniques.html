Prompt Engineering, Finetuning, RAG

AI Comment

Large Language Models (LLMs) are powerful, but vulnerable to hallucinationgenerating inaccurate or invented outputs, particularly when faced with unfamiliar data or ambiguous queries. Three main strategies help adapt models and manage these risks:

  • Prompt Engineering: Requires no additional training; carefully crafted text prompts can steer LLMs toward more reliable and specific results, addressing hallucination or ambiguity.
  • Finetuning: Retrains LLMs on custom datasets, altering model weights to specialize in certain domains. This produces a tailored model with better factual grounding for a target application.
  • RAG: Augments models with external knowledge retrieval, enabling accurate Q&A and document summarization by referencing up-to-date information. No retraining is needed.
These techniques can be used individually or in combination, supporting use cases from chatbots to complex research. Effective adaptation helps mitigate LLM weaknesses and maximize their practical benefit for both generic and specialized tasks.
